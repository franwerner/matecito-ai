# Explore variations

For the divergent stage: instead of polishing a single idea, open the fan and put real options
on the table for the person to choose from.

## When it applies

- Kicking off a brand identity (several directions).
- Designing a screen/piece with no defined direction (several styles or layouts).
- When the person is stuck on a single idea and contrasting it helps.

## Flow

1. **Understand the goal and constraints**: what is being designed, for whom, what CANNOT be
   missing, and whether a brand guide already exists (if it does, variations respect the brand; if
   it's new-brand exploration, they don't).
2. **Propose 3-4 GENUINELY distinct directions**, not the same idea in another color. Each with: a
   short name, the concept in one sentence, and how it differs.
3. **Show trade-offs**: what each direction prioritizes and what it gives up (e.g. "bolder but less
   legible", "safer but less memorable").
4. **If generation tools exist** (Canva, image MCP), materialize the directions as visual sketches;
   if not, describe them precisely enough to picture (palette, type, layout, mood).
5. **Converge**: when the person chooses, pass the winning direction to the right skill
   (`brand-guide` to lock it, `generate-assets` to produce).

## Rules

- Real variety: if two options look too similar, merge them and bring a distinct third.
- Don't decide for the person: present and recommend with arguments, but the choice is theirs.
- Don't fall in love with one option or push it; your job is to open the space.

## Mentor mode

Explain the why behind each decision in 1-2 lines — the design principle, not just the what.
Derive to the `explain-concept` skill for unfamiliar concepts. (Full rule in the domain CLAUDE.md.)
